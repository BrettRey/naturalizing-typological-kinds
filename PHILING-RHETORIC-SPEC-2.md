# Phil-Ling Rhetoric Analyzer: Eagle & Mouse Protocol

## Core Principle
**Diagnose first, then surface natural adjustments.** Use Cicero, Aristotle, Shakespeare, Macagno & Walton, Katja Thieme, and João Lemos as *analytical frameworks* to reveal how a paper *already* persuades. Recommendations should fall out of the analysis like loose threads—not as templates to copy, but as structural tensions to resolve or amplify.

---

## Input
The agent reads `main.tex` as the target paper.

---

## Analysis Protocol

### Phase 1: EAGLE'S VIEW (Macro-Structure)
*Map the architecture. What holds this paper aloft?*

#### 1.1 Ciceronian Dispositio & Academic Ethos
Using **Cicero's Arrangement** as a lens, dissect the paper's global structure:
- **Inventio**: List the *types* of arguments deployed (empirical, conceptual, historical). Which are foregrounded? Which are smuggled in as assumptions?
- **Dispositio**: Chart the sequence. Where does the paper execute the "turn" (a la Obama)? Identify the moment where Introduction → Body → Conclusion shifts from summary to stake-raising. Tag the paragraph number.
- **Ethos Construction**: How is scholarly authority established in the first 500 words? (Citations, methodological claims, problem formulation?) Locate the precise sentence where the author positions themselves as *necessary* to the debate—not just competent.

**Recommendation**: If the turn arrives too late (after para 8), consider whether the paper is front-loading credentials and burying its unique thesis. The fix isn't "sound like Cicero"—it's "let the dispositio match your actual argument's urgency."

#### 1.2 Aristotelian Appeals in Academic Drag
- **Logos**: Identify the *dominant logical structure* (deductive syllogism, inductive case-study matrix, abductive inference to best explanation). Is the logic explicit or buried under metalanguage?
- **Pathos**: Find the *stealth affective moment*. Academic papers ban emotion, but where does word choice (e.g., "catastrophic oversight," "elegant solution") smuggle in urgency or admiration? Quote it.
- **Ethos**: Beyond credentialing, where does the paper perform intellectual generosity or rigor? (e.g., "Admittedly, this approach costs us..." — a concession that strengthens authority).

**Recommendation**: If pathos appears only in the conclusion's call-to-action, the paper may be under-signaling stakes. The fix isn't "add emotion"—it's "distribute urgency across the logical chain so readers feel the cost of each premise."

#### 1.3 Genre Occlusion & Uptake (Thieme's Lens)
- **Genre Markers**: Categorize the paper: Is it a "problem-solution," "state-of-the-field," or "methodological intervention"? Cite 2-3 phrases that signal this to editors.
- **Occlusion Points**: What does the paper *hide* about its own construction? (e.g., selection of case studies buried in a footnote; theoretical commitments framed as "intuitions"). List 3 hidden moves.
- **Anticipated Uptake**: Who is the imagined reader? Another philosopher, a linguist, or a cross-disciplinary bridge-builder? Identify the sentence that *scripts* the reader's response (e.g., "This suggests that..." — what does it *command* the reader to see?).

**Recommendation**: If you've occluded your case selection criteria, the recommendation isn't "be transparent like Thieme"—it's "move the selection rationale into the main body, not to be virtuous, but because hiding it makes your argument from expert opinion vulnerable to 'cherry-picking' critiques."

#### 1.4 Argumentation Scheme Architecture (Macagno & Walton)
Map the paper's top-level moves onto their schemes:
- **Argument from Expert Opinion**: "As Chomsky (1995) showed..." — locate and evaluate how the authority is *framed*.
- **Argument from Analogy**: Find the analogical hinge in the argument. Is it explicit ("Similarly...") or structural (the paper *maps* one domain onto another)?
- **Pragmatic Argument**: Does the paper argue that accepting its claim enables better research? Quote the "if-then" structure.

**Recommendation**: If your argument from analogy hinges on a single structural mapping, the fix isn't "use more analogies"—it's "stress-test the mapping in a dedicated paragraph: what breaks if the analogy is pushed?"

### Phase 2: MOUSE'S VIEW (Micro-Moves)
*Crawl through the syntax. What persuades at the level of the clause?*

#### 2.1 Shakespearean Structural Integration
For each rhetorical figure found, **do not** praise it. Instead, show how the figure *is* the argument:
- **Asyndeton (omitted conjunctions)**: Find a list where removing "and" or "but" forces conceptual compression. Quote it. Ask: What does this *urgency* prevent the reader from questioning?
- **Antimetabole/Chiasmus**: Locate an A-B-B-A structure (e.g., "Philosophy analyzes language; language, in turn, constrains philosophy"). Show how the reversal *performs* the theoretical claim.
- **Polyptoton** (repeating a root word with different endings): "We *analyze* the *analyst's* own *analyses*..." — find this. What does the morphological echo smuggle in as settled?

**Recommendation**: If asyndeton appears in your methodology section, you're potentially rushing past a weakness. The fix isn't "add conjunctions"—it's "decide whether that urgency is genuine (the method is simple) or defensive (the method is shaky)."

#### 2.2 Rhythm & the Lemosian "Clarity Trap"
- **Sentence Length Variation**: Extract the shortest and longest sentences in the abstract. What conceptual work does the *rhythm* perform? (Short = axiom; long = qualification that neutralizes critique).
- **Parataxis vs. Hypotaxis**: Identify a moment of flat parataxis ("X is true. Y follows. Z results.") where hypotaxis would normally be expected ("If X, then Y, which entails Z..."). What does the syntax hide about causality?
- **Occlusive Metalanguage** (Thieme/Krieg-Planque): Highlight every "indeed," "in fact," "basically," "as it were." For each, diagnose: Is this a hedge, a intensifier, or a genre-compliance marker?

**Recommendation**: If your abstract's longest sentence is a 60-word hedge, the fix isn't "write shorter sentences"—it's "distribute that hedge across the paper's body so the abstract can state the thesis cleanly."

#### 2.3 Lexical Collusion
- **Signature Collocations**: What two-word phrase appears &gt;3 times? (e.g., "discursive practice," "semantic scaffolding"). What conceptual package does this *repeat* into plausibility?
- **Stealth Theorizing**: Find a noun phrase that *is* a theory in disguise (e.g., "the natural reading," "our intuitive grasp") — where the definite article ("the") or possessive ("our") smuggles in universality.

**Recommendation**: If "discursive practice" appears 8 times without definition, the fix isn't "vary your vocabulary"—it's "recognize that you're using repetition as a placeholder for argument. Either define it once as technical term, or show how each instance shifts meaning."

#### 2.4 The Mouse's Critical Question
For every micro-find, ask: **Does this device enable thought, or seal it shut?** Provide a verdict: "Open" (invites interrogation) or "Closed" (pre-empts it).

**Recommendation**: If the verdict is "Closed" in your results section, the recommendation isn't "delete the device"—it's "reframe it as a hypothesis: 'This suggests that...' becomes 'One possible reading is...', opening space for counter-readings."

###**Mouse's view trove**
**Figure-as-Argument**: [Device] in "[quote]" → performs [conceptual work] → Verdict: Open/Closed

**Rhythm Diagnosis**: Short/Long sentence pair → creates [effect]

**Metalanguage**: "[word]" at [location] → functions as [hedge/intensifier/script]

**Mouse Recommendation**: [One sentence: "Consider [specific local change] because [analytical reason], not to imitate [rhetorician]."]

###**Synthesis Verdict**
**Rhetorical Strength**: [What the paper's structure *uniquely* enables]

**Structural Risk**: [Where a device might collapse under scrutiny]

**Synthesis Recommendation**: [One integrated suggestion that links eagle and mouse findings: e.g., "The delayed turn (Eagle) and defensive asyndeton (Mouse) both suggest the paper is hiding its originality. Consider moving your core thesis to paragraph 2 and letting the methodology section's parataxis be a confident statement of protocol, not a rushed dodge."]

###**Final Principle**
**No Imitation Advised:** The masters are diagnostic lenses, not templates. Every recommendation must be "...because this serves your argument's conditions, not because Cicero/Shakespeare/Macagno did it." Don't literally append that, but it should be appendable.
