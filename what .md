<meta http-equiv="content-type" content="text/html; charset=windows-1252"></head><body>---<br>title: Phil-Ling Rhetoric Analyzer<br>description: Dual-perspective rhetorical analysis for philosophy-linguistics papers with emergent recommendations<br>version: 1.1<br>author: Kimi CLI Agent<br>input: main.tex<br>---<br><br># Phil-Ling Rhetoric Analyzer: Eagle &amp; Mouse Protocol<br><br>## Core Principle<br>**Diagnose
 first, then surface natural adjustments.** Use Cicero, Aristotle, 
Shakespeare, Macagno &amp; Walton, Katja Thieme, and João Lemos as 
*analytical frameworks* to reveal how a paper *already* persuades. 
Recommendations should fall out of the analysis like loose threads—not 
as templates to copy, but as structural tensions to resolve or amplify.<br><br>---<br><br>## Input<br>The agent reads `main.tex` as the target paper.<br><br>---<br><br>## Analysis Protocol<br><br>### Phase 1: EAGLE'S VIEW (Macro-Structure)<br>*Map the architecture. What holds this paper aloft?*<br><br>#### 1.1 Ciceronian Dispositio &amp; Academic Ethos<br>Using **Cicero's Arrangement** as a lens, dissect the paper's global structure:<br>-
 **Inventio**: List the *types* of arguments deployed (empirical, 
conceptual, historical). Which are foregrounded? Which are smuggled in 
as assumptions?<br>- **Dispositio**: Chart the sequence. Where does the 
paper execute the "turn" (a la Obama)? Identify the moment where 
Introduction &#8594; Body &#8594; Conclusion shifts from summary to stake-raising. 
Tag the paragraph number.<br>- **Ethos Construction**: How is scholarly 
authority established in the first 500 words? (Citations, methodological
 claims, problem formulation?) Locate the precise sentence where the 
author positions themselves as *necessary* to the debate—not just 
competent.<br><br>**Recommendation**: If the turn arrives too late 
(after para 8), consider whether the paper is front-loading credentials 
and burying its unique thesis. The fix isn't "sound like Cicero"—it's 
"let the dispositio match your actual argument's urgency."<br><br>#### 1.2 Aristotelian Appeals in Academic Drag<br>-
 **Logos**: Identify the *dominant logical structure* (deductive 
syllogism, inductive case-study matrix, abductive inference to best 
explanation). Is the logic explicit or buried under metalanguage?<br>- 
**Pathos**: Find the *stealth affective moment*. Academic papers ban 
emotion, but where does word choice (e.g., "catastrophic oversight," 
"elegant solution") smuggle in urgency or admiration? Quote it.<br>- 
**Ethos**: Beyond credentialing, where does the paper perform 
intellectual generosity or rigor? (e.g., "Admittedly, this approach 
costs us..." — a concession that strengthens authority).<br><br>**Recommendation**:
 If pathos appears only in the conclusion's call-to-action, the paper 
may be under-signaling stakes. The fix isn't "add emotion"—it's 
"distribute urgency across the logical chain so readers feel the cost of
 each premise."<br><br>#### 1.3 Genre Occlusion &amp; Uptake (Thieme's Lens)<br>-
 **Genre Markers**: Categorize the paper: Is it a "problem-solution," 
"state-of-the-field," or "methodological intervention"? Cite 2-3 phrases
 that signal this to editors.<br>- **Occlusion Points**: What does the 
paper *hide* about its own construction? (e.g., selection of case 
studies buried in a footnote; theoretical commitments framed as 
"intuitions"). List 3 hidden moves.<br>- **Anticipated Uptake**: Who is 
the imagined reader? Another philosopher, a linguist, or a 
cross-disciplinary bridge-builder? Identify the sentence that *scripts* 
the reader's response (e.g., "This suggests that..." — what does it 
*command* the reader to see?).<br><br>**Recommendation**: If you've 
occluded your case selection criteria, the recommendation isn't "be 
transparent like Thieme"—it's "move the selection rationale into the 
main body, not to be virtuous, but because hiding it makes your argument
 from expert opinion vulnerable to 'cherry-picking' critiques."<br><br>#### 1.4 Argumentation Scheme Architecture (Macagno &amp; Walton)<br>Map the paper's top-level moves onto their schemes:<br>- **Argument from Expert Opinion**: "As Chomsky (1995) showed..." — locate and evaluate how the authority is *framed*.<br>-
 **Argument from Analogy**: Find the analogical hinge in the argument. 
Is it explicit ("Similarly...") or structural (the paper *maps* one 
domain onto another)?<br>- **Pragmatic Argument**: Does the paper argue that accepting its claim enables better research? Quote the "if-then" structure.<br><br>**Recommendation**:
 If your argument from analogy hinges on a single structural mapping, 
the fix isn't "use more analogies"—it's "stress-test the mapping in a 
dedicated paragraph: what breaks if the analogy is pushed?"<br><br>### Phase 2: MOUSE'S VIEW (Micro-Moves)<br>*Crawl through the syntax. What persuades at the level of the clause?*<br><br>#### 2.1 Shakespearean Structural Integration<br>For each rhetorical figure found, **do not** praise it. Instead, show how the figure *is* the argument:<br>-
 **Asyndeton (omitted conjunctions)**: Find a list where removing "and" 
or "but" forces conceptual compression. Quote it. Ask: What does this 
*urgency* prevent the reader from questioning?<br>- 
**Antimetabole/Chiasmus**: Locate an A-B-B-A structure (e.g., 
"Philosophy analyzes language; language, in turn, constrains 
philosophy"). Show how the reversal *performs* the theoretical claim.<br>-
 **Polyptoton** (repeating a root word with different endings): "We 
*analyze* the *analyst's* own *analyses*..." — find this. What does the 
morphological echo smuggle in as settled?<br><br>**Recommendation**: If 
asyndeton appears in your methodology section, you're potentially 
rushing past a weakness. The fix isn't "add conjunctions"—it's "decide 
whether that urgency is genuine (the method is simple) or defensive (the
 method is shaky)."<br><br>#### 2.2 Rhythm &amp; the Lemosian "Clarity Trap"<br>-
 **Sentence Length Variation**: Extract the shortest and longest 
sentences in the abstract. What conceptual work does the *rhythm* 
perform? (Short = axiom; long = qualification that neutralizes 
critique).<br>- **Parataxis vs. Hypotaxis**: Identify a moment of flat 
parataxis ("X is true. Y follows. Z results.") where hypotaxis would 
normally be expected ("If X, then Y, which entails Z..."). What does the
 syntax hide about causality?<br>- **Occlusive Metalanguage** 
(Thieme/Krieg-Planque): Highlight every "indeed," "in fact," 
"basically," "as it were." For each, diagnose: Is this a hedge, a 
intensifier, or a genre-compliance marker?<br><br>**Recommendation**: If
 your abstract's longest sentence is a 60-word hedge, the fix isn't 
"write shorter sentences"—it's "distribute that hedge across the paper's
 body so the abstract can state the thesis cleanly."<br><br>#### 2.3 Lexical Collusion<br>-
 **Signature Collocations**: What two-word phrase appears &amp;gt;3 
times? (e.g., "discursive practice," "semantic scaffolding"). What 
conceptual package does this *repeat* into plausibility?<br>- **Stealth 
Theorizing**: Find a noun phrase that *is* a theory in disguise (e.g., 
"the natural reading," "our intuitive grasp") — where the definite 
article ("the") or possessive ("our") smuggles in universality.<br><br>**Recommendation**:
 If "discursive practice" appears 8 times without definition, the fix 
isn't "vary your vocabulary"—it's "recognize that you're using 
repetition as a placeholder for argument. Either define it once as 
technical term, or show how each instance shifts meaning."<br><br>#### 2.4 The Mouse's Critical Question<br>For
 every micro-find, ask: **Does this device enable thought, or seal it 
shut?** Provide a verdict: "Open" (invites interrogation) or "Closed" 
(pre-empts it).<br><br>**Recommendation**: If the verdict is "Closed" in
 your results section, the recommendation isn't "delete the device"—it's
 "reframe it as a hypothesis: 'This suggests that...' becomes 'One 
possible reading is...', opening space for counter-readings."<br><br>---<br><br>## Output Format<br><br>### **Eagle's View Summary**<br>**Thesis**: [One-sentence summary of the paper's core claim]<br><br>**Ciceronian Arc**:<br>- Inventio: [Argument types]<br>- Dispositio: [Turn location and function]<br>- Ethos Pivot: [Quote from intro]<br><br>**Genre &amp; Occlusion**:<br>- Type: [category]<br>- Hidden: [3 occluded moves]<br>- Reader Script: [Quote and uptake command]<br><br><div>**Eagle Recommendation**: [One paragraph on macro-structural tension to resolve]</div><div><br></div><div>###**Mouse's view trove**</div><div>**Figure-as-Argument**: [Device] in "[quote]" &#8594; performs [conceptual work] &#8594; Verdict: Open/Closed<br><br>**Rhythm Diagnosis**: Short/Long sentence pair &#8594; creates [effect]<br><br>**Metalanguage**: "[word]" at [location] &#8594; functions as [hedge/intensifier/script]<br><br>**Mouse
